## Purpose
This catalog turns Smart Learn's motivation framework into reusable response patterns.

The point is not to make feedback robotic. The point is to ensure the system stays supportive, honest, and consistent across learn, review, quiz, and reinforce flows.

---

## Template design principles
- Praise effort, focus, persistence, and improvement before raw correctness alone.
- Keep encouragement specific whenever possible.
- Avoid shame, sarcasm, or exaggerated hype.
- End feedback with a helpful next step when the learner is not fully correct.
- Vary wording so praise does not feel copy-pasted.

## Encouraging feedback patterns

### 1. Correct answer, confident performance
Use when the learner answers correctly with clear understanding.

Patterns:
- "Nice work — you got the core idea right."
- "Yes — that’s correct, and your reasoning is solid."
- "Exactly. You’re showing real control of this concept."
- "That’s right. You didn’t just guess it — you understood it."

Follow-up options:
- "Let’s build on that with a slightly harder one."
- "Want to move to the next concept?"

### 2. Correct answer after hesitation
Use when the learner gets it right but looked unsure.

Patterns:
- "You got there — nice job staying with it."
- "Correct. Even with some hesitation, you worked it through."
- "That’s right. Taking a moment to think was the right move."
- "Good catch — you recovered and landed on the right idea."

Follow-up options:
- "Let’s do one more to make it feel steadier."
- "You’re close to making this automatic."

### 3. Partially correct answer
Use when the learner has part of the concept but not the full picture.

Patterns:
- "You’ve got part of it — now let’s complete the picture."
- "Good start. The main idea is there, but one piece needs adjustment."
- "You’re thinking in the right direction. Let’s tighten one detail."
- "That answer shows progress — we just need one correction."

Follow-up options:
- "Here’s the missing piece..."
- "Try again using this hint."

### 4. Strong effort despite incorrect answer
Use when the learner is wrong but clearly engaging thoughtfully.

Patterns:
- "Good effort — I can see what you were trying to do."
- "Not quite, but your reasoning had a useful starting point."
- "That wasn’t the right answer, but it was a serious attempt."
- "You’re engaging with the concept, which matters — let’s refine it."

Follow-up options:
- "Here’s where the idea bends away from the correct path..."
- "Let’s break it into a smaller step."

### 5. Improvement compared with prior attempts
Use when performance is getting better over time.

Patterns:
- "You’re improving — this is clearly stronger than your last attempt."
- "That’s progress. More of the concept is sticking now."
- "Nice — your understanding is moving in the right direction."
- "You’re building momentum here."

Follow-up options:
- "Let’s reinforce it once more so it stays stable."
- "One more clean pass and this may shift into a strength."

### 6. Persistence after difficulty
Use when the learner kept going through struggle.

Patterns:
- "Nice persistence — you stayed with a hard concept."
- "That took effort, and you didn’t bail out. That matters."
- "Good resilience. Working through difficulty is real progress."
- "You kept going, and that’s exactly how this gets easier over time."

Follow-up options:
- "Let’s make the next step smaller."
- "You’ve earned a simpler reinforcement pass before we raise the difficulty again."

### 7. Focus and completion praise
Use when the learner completes a useful unit of work.

Patterns:
- "You finished the block cleanly — well done."
- "Nice focus. You stayed on track and completed the step."
- "That was a solid study block."
- "Good work — you closed the loop instead of drifting away from it."

Follow-up options:
- "Ready for the next topic?"
- "Want a short review before moving on?"

## Variation strategy
To avoid repetitive tone, the system should vary feedback along these dimensions:
- recognition of correctness
- recognition of effort
- recognition of progress over time
- next-step coaching
- emotional steadiness during struggle

A single feedback message does not need all five. Usually two is enough.

---

## Mode-aware usage notes
- **Learn:** keep praise light and momentum-friendly.
- **Review:** emphasize recall strengthening and rebuilding confidence.
- **Quiz:** be precise and fair; praise correctness or effort without sounding inflated.
- **Reinforce:** emphasize safety, progress, and recoverability.

---

## Anti-patterns
Do not use templates like:
- "Wrong again."
- "This should be easy."
- "You failed this."
- "No, that makes no sense."
- "Amazing!!!" for ordinary performance

The catalog should protect honesty and dignity at the same time.
